Academics

In my academic journey, I've taken several courses that sparked my curiosity. These are a few of the classes I took which interested me.

Calculus 1, 2 & 3 (University of Utah)

Through these courses, I gained a strong understanding in differential and integral calculus, sequences and series, and multivariable calculus. Working through complex problems and real-world applications strengthened both my analytical thinking and my ability to model mathematical relationships in science and other areas of life.

Foundations of Biology (University of Utah)

This course introduced me to the foundations of biochemistry and how life works on the molecular and cellular levels. Learning about this made me really excited for more advanced biology courses.

Physics for Scientists and Engineers 1 & 2 (University of Utah)

These courses focused on classical mechanics, electricity, and magnetism, combining theoretical lessons with hard problem-solving exercises.

Chemistry Honors (High School)

As one of my first rigorous science classes, Chemistry Honors challenged me and marked my first real experience in a laboratory setting. I learned to approach experiments methodically, work safely with chemicals, and analyze results critically.

General Chemistry 1 (University of Utah)

After learning chemistry in high school, this course deepened my understanding on all the different chemistry topics such as atomic structure, periodic trends, bonding, stoichiometry, thermochemistry, and more.

Art of Engineering (High School)

This course gave me a new perspective on engineering and architecture, allowing me to explore how structures are designed and built. I grew an appreciation for the creativity involved in engineering and this class provided an outlet to combine artistic expression with analytical thinking. I enjoyed designing projects that balanced function, aesthetics, and innovation.
