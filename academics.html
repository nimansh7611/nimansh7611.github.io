Academics

In my academic journey, I've taken several courses that sparked my curiosity. These are a few of the classes I took which interested me.

Chemistry Honors

As one of my first rigorous science classes, Chemistry Honors challenged me and marked my first real experience in a laboratory setting. I learned to approach experiments methodically, work safely with chemicals, and analyze results critically. This course helped me develop a strong foundation in scientific thinking and practical lab skills.

Physics for Scientists and Engineers 1 & 2 (University of Utah)

These courses focused on classical mechanics, electricity, and magnetism, combining theoretical lessons with hard problem-solving exercises.

Calculus 1, 2 & 3 (University of Utah)

Through these courses, I gained a strong understanding in differential and integral calculus, sequences and series, and multivariable calculus. Working through complex problems and real-world applications strengthened both my analytical thinking and my ability to model mathematical relationships in science and finance.

Art of Engineering

This course gave me a new perspective on engineering and architecture, allowing me to explore how structures are designed and built. I grew an appreciation for the creativity involved in engineering and this class provided an outlet to combine artistic expression with analytical thinking. I enjoyed designing projects that balanced function, aesthetics, and innovation.
